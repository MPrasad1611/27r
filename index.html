AboutUs

A website is a collection of web pages and related content that is accessible through a web address or domain name. Websites are typically created by a person or organization and are dedicated to a specific topic or purpose, such as news, entertainment, or commerce.

For the Internet domain, see .website. For the on-demand archiving service, see WebCite. The usap.gov website A website (also written as a web site) is one or more web pages and related content that is identified by a common domain name and published on at least one web server. Websites are typically dedicated to a particular topic or purpose, such as news, education, commerce, entertainment, or social media. Hyperlinking between web pages guides the navigation of the site, which often starts with a home page. The most-visited sites are Google, YouTube, and Facebook. All publicly-accessible websites collectively constitute the World Wide Web. There are also private websites that can only be accessed on a private network, such as a company's internal website for its employees. Users can access websites on a range of devices, including desktops, laptops, tablets, and smartphones. The app used on these devices is called a web browser.
